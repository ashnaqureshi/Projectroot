The Rethink Opioids initiative was developed through the collaboration of a Steering Committee and the sponsor of the initiative, Pfizer Inc. This collaborative effort is referred to as PROJECT ROOT.

The PROJECT ROOT Steering Committee is a diverse, distinguished panel of professionals who seek to make a real difference with respect to the problem of prescription opioid abuse. Comprising clinicians, academicians, and patient advocates, the Committee met to discuss the scope of the problem and, importantly, to begin developing practical approaches to the problem of opioid prescribing for patients with chronic pain. The information, tools, and resources you will find here are a product of the Steering Committee’s work and their collaboration with the sponsor of the initiative, Pfizer Inc.

Select a Steering Committee member to view a brief biography, and watch this brief video to hear from Committee members regarding why they joined PROJECT ROOT.

*The PROJECT ROOT Steering Committee members or their organizations are compensated by Pfizer for their participation.

Charles Argoff, MD

Dr Argoff is professor of neurology at Albany Medical College and director of the Comprehensive Pain Program at Albany Medical Center in New York. Specializing in pain management and chronic headache, Dr Argoff predominately treats patients with chronic and neuropathic pain. He is an active member of several professional organizations, including the American Academy of Neurology, the International Association for the Study of Pain, the American Academy of Pain Medicine and the American Pain Society, among others. In addition, he is president of the American Academy of Pain Medicine Foundation Board. Dr Argoff is a frequent contributor to the medical literature and had an active role in the development of the diabetic peripheral neuropathic pain guidelines published in Mayo Clinic Proceedings . He serves on the editorial board of The Clinical Journal of Pain and is a reviewer for Journal of Pain, Brain, Journal of the American Medical Association, Archives of Physical Medicine and Rehabilitation, Journal of Musculoskeletal Pain, Journal of Pain and Symptom Management , and The Clinical Journal of Pain . In addition, he is coeditor of the Neuropathic Pain section for Pain Medicine ; one of the editors of Raj’s Practical Management of Pain , 4th edition; coeditor of Pain Management Secrets , 3rd edition; and coauthor of Defeat Chronic Pain Now , a book written for people with chronic pain.

Bill H. McCarberg, MD

Dr McCarberg is Founder of the Chronic Pain Management Program for Kaiser Permanente (retired) in San Diego, California. Currently, he is working with Neighborhood Healthcare in Escondido, California. He is president-elect of the American Academy of Pain Medicine. Dr McCarberg is president of the Western Pain Society and adjunct assistant clinical professor at the University of California, San Diego School of Medicine. He is a member of the American Academy of Family Physicians, American Academy of Pain Medicine, American Pain Society, and International Association for the Study of Pain. He is the recipient of several awards including the Shilling Compassionate Care Award, the Elizabeth Narcessian award for leader in the field of pain education from the American Pain Society, and in 2008 was Ambassador of the Year for the National Pain Foundation. Member Appraisal of Physician Services at Kaiser Permanente rated Dr McCarberg in the top 10% from 1998-2011. He has given more than 250 presentations on pain management issues and is the author or coauthor of 115 articles, book chapters, and books. He is board certified by the American College of Pain Medicine and the American Board of Family Practice and additionally certified in geriatrics. Dr McCarberg received his medical degree from Northwestern University Medical School in Chicago. He completed a medical internship and a residency in family practice at Highland Hospital in Rochester, New York.

Marcia Lee Taylor

Ms Taylor is Senior Vice President, Director of Government Affairs for a national nonprofit, Partnership for Drug-Free Kids. Before joining Partnership for Drug-Free Kids, Ms Taylor worked as a staffer on Capitol Hill for 11 years, serving as the senior drug policy advisor to the Senate Judiciary Subcommittee on Crime and Drugs. In this role she worked on many important pieces of legislation including the Drug Addiction Treatment Act, which amended a long-standing statute—the Narcotic Addicts Treatment Act—to allow qualified physicians to prescribe certain new anti-addiction medications in their offices rather than requiring patients to go to specialized clinics for treatment. Currently, Ms Taylor heads The Medicine Abuse Project, a multiyear campaign led by The Partnership at Drugfree.org that aims to prevent half a million teens from abusing medicine by the year 2017. In addition, Ms Taylor is a board member and secretary for the US Anti-Doping Agency, the official anti-doping organization for Olympic, Pan American and Paralympic sport in the United States.

Penney Cowan

Ms Cowan is the founder and executive director of the American Chronic Pain Association in Rocklin, California. She began the Partners for Understanding Pain campaign in an attempt to raise awareness about the need to better understand, assess, and treat people with pain. The campaign, under the direction of the American Chronic Pain Association, successfully established September as Pain Awareness Month. Ms Cowan is a member of the patient leadership council for the Clinical Trials Transformation Initiative and has served as a co-chair of the self-management working group for the steering committee of the Osteoarthritis Alliance. She currently serves on the Interagency Pain Research Coordinating Committee of the National Institutes of Health until July 2016 and is co-chair of Public Education and Communication Committee for the National Pain Strategies working group. She was a consumer representative for the FDA/CDER Anesthetic and Analgesic Drug Products Advisory Committee, served as a member of the steering committee for the Chronic Pain Network, and was a steering committee member and vice president of the Neuropathic Pain Network. She has received numerous awards including the Jefferson Medal for Outstanding Citizen, the John and Emma Bonica Public Service Award, a presidential commendation from the American Academy of Pain Medicine, and the Elizabeth Narcessian Award for Outstanding Educational Achievements from the American Pain Society.

Sandy Robertson, PharmD

Dr Robertson is a clinical pharmacy specialist at the Cabarrus Family Medicine Residency Program at the Carolinas HealthCare System in Concord, North Carolina. Her areas of interest include women's health and anticoagulation. As a member of the Chronic Pain Task Force at Cabarrus Family Medicine, she is involved with creating a chronic pain management resource within the electronic medical record system, updating policy for the appropriate use of opioids, and transforming the management of chronic pain for all practice sites. Additionally, Dr Robertson is involved with chronic disease management education, in which she provides one-on-one teaching sessions with Family Medicine residents. She is also involved with reviewing evidence-based guidelines for diabetes, asthma, chronic obstructive pulmonary disease, coronary artery disease, hypertension, and chronic pain, and reviews the resident patient panels for these conditions to ensure that appropriate care is provided.

Yvonne D’Arcy, MS, CRNP, CNS

Ms D’Arcy is a nurse practitioner at Suburban Hospital–Johns Hopkins Medicine in Bethesda, Maryland. She specializes in pain management and palliative care. Ms D’Arcy lectures nationally on subjects such as chronic pain, neuropathic pain syndrome, and acute pain management. She is a past member of the American Pain Society’s Clinical Guidelines Committee. Ms D’Arcy served on the board of directors and as chair of the Clinical Practice Committee of the American Society for Pain Management Nursing. She is an editorial advisory board member for Nursing, The Journal for Nurse Practitioners, and Pain Medicine News. Ms D’Arcy has written numerous articles for journals and chapters for nursing textbooks. She was an Eddie winner for the article, “How to Manage Pain in Addicted Patients,” and received the American Journal of Nursing Book of the Year Award for How to Manage Pain in the Elderly in 2010. Additionally, Ms D’Arcy’s book Compact Clinical Guide to Cancer Pain Management: An Evidence-Based Approach for Nurses , coauthored with Pamela Davies, MS, ARNP, was selected by the American Journal of Nursing as a 2013 Book of the Year. Ms D’Arcy is a series editor for the Compact Clinical Guide to Pain Management and has authored or coauthored 5 books in this series. Her latest book, A Compact Clinical Guide to Women’s Pain Management , was published in September 2013. Ms D’Arcy is an evidence-based practice mentor and is active in clinical research. She is also the recipient of the Maryland, Virginia, and Washington, DC, Nursing Spectrum Nursing Excellence Award for Advancing and Leading the Profession.

Steven P. Stanos, Jr, DO

Dr Stanos is Medical Director, Occupational Medicine Services, at Swedish Medical Center in Seattle. Previously, he was the Director of Corporate Pain Services at the Rehabilitation Institute of Chicago (RIC) and Medical Director of the Center for Pain Management at RIC. Dr Stanos serves on the board of directors of the American Academy of Pain Medicine and is active in the American Pain Society, the Midwest Pain Society, and the American Chronic Pain Association. He is also a member of the American Academy of Physical Medicine and Rehabilitation (AAPM&R), for which he has served as Vice Chair of Education on the Pain Medicine/Neuromuscular Medicine Council as well as the Opioid REMS Task Force. Dr Stanos has participated in the publication of a number of pain treatment guidelines and as an invited reviewer for the American Pain Society’s opioid management guidelines. Dr Stanos lectures extensively nationally and abroad on various topics related to pain assessment, pain rehabilitation, and pharmacotherapy. In 2013, Dr Stanos was awarded a Mayday Pain and Society Fellowship, for which his project will focus on increasing awareness and coverage for interdisciplinary pain rehabilitation treatment. In 2010, he was the recipient of the AAPM&R Service Award for the Pain Medicine/Neuromuscular Rehabilitation Council. He has also received the Knee Society Ranawat Award of the American Academy of Orthopaedic Surgeons.
